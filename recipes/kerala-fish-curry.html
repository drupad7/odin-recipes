Kerala Fish Curry

Description

A fiery red, tangy fish curry cooked in a traditional clay pot using Malabar tamarind (Kudampuli) and sharp spices.

Ingredients

  • Fish
  • Malabar Tamarind (Kudampuli)
  • Shallots
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Spices
  • Tempering
    •

Steps

  1. Prep Tamarind: Soak the Kudampuli pieces in warm water with a pinch of salt.
  2. Splutter Seeds: Heat coconut oil in a clay pot (Manchatti) and splutter mustard seeds and fenugreek seeds.
  3. Sauté Base: Add shallots, ginger, garlic, and curry leaves, frying until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Make Paste: Mix chili powder and turmeric with a splash of water to form a paste, then stir it into the pot on low heat.
  5. Add Wagit push origin mainter: Pour in the soaked tamarind along with its soaking water and an extra cup of water.
  6. Simmer: Bring the gravy to a boil and let it simmer for 5 minutes to develop flavors.
  7. Cook Fish: Gently slide the fish pieces into the boiling gravy, cover, and cook on medium heat for 12 minutes.
  8. Set: Remove from heat, swirl the pot gently, sprinkle fenugreek powder, and let it rest for 2 hours before serving.
