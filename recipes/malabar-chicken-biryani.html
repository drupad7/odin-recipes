Malabar Chicken Biryani

Description

A legendary, fragrant rice dish made with thin Khaima rice, mild spices, and a unique slow-cooking Dum method.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Khaima/Jeerakasala Rice
  • Onions
  • Green Chili-Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • Garnishes
  • Fat
    •

Steps

  1. Fry Garnishes: Heat ghee and oil to fry cashews, raisins, and half of the sliced onions until golden brown, then set aside.
  2. Cook Chicken Masala: Sauté the remaining onions, green chili-ginger-garlic paste, and chopped tomatoes until soft.
  3. Simmer Chicken: Add the chicken, a pinch of turmeric, garam masala, yogurt, mint, and coriander leaves, cooking covered until tender.
  4. Cook Rice: Boil water with whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon), add rice, and cook until it is 80% done, then drain.
  5. Layer Masala: In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread the cooked chicken masala evenly as the base layer.
  6. Layer Rice: Spread the parboiled rice over the chicken masala.
  7. Garnish Layers: Top the rice with the fried onions, cashews, raisins, chopped herbs, and saffron milk.
  8. Dum Cook: Seal the pot tightly with dough or foil, place the lid on top, and cook on low heat for 15 minutes.
