Alfam

Description

Alfam is a popular Arabian-style grilled chicken dish known for its smoky flavor and tender texture. The chicken is marinated in a vibrant blend of yogurt, lemon juice, garlic, ginger, and traditional Arabic spices. It is then slow-grilled over charcoal, which creates a beautifully charred exterior while keeping the meat juicy inside. This flavorful dish is traditionally served hot alongside kubboos flatbread, creamy garlic paste, and fresh salad.

Steps

  1. Prep Chicken: Wash 1 kg of large chicken pieces and cut deep slits into the flesh.
  2. Dry Meat: Pat the chicken pieces completely dry using clean paper towels.
  3. Blend Aromatics: Grind onion, tomato, garlic, ginger, and green chilies into a smooth, waterless paste.
  4. Mix Marinade: Whisk the blended paste with yogurt, lemon juice, oil, and all spice powders.
  5. Apply Spices: Rub the marinade thoroughly over the chicken, forcing it deep into the slits.
  6. Marinate: Cover the chicken and refrigerate for a minimum of 4 hours to absorb flavors.
  7. Preheat: Bring the chicken to room temperature and preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) or fire up charcoal.
  8. Grill/Bake: Cook the chicken for 35 to 40 minutes, flipping it halfway through the cooking time.
  9. Char Skin: Broil in the oven for 3 minutes or baste with oil over charcoal for a smoky finish.
  10. Rest and Serve: Let the meat rest for 5 minutes before serving with hot flatbread and garlic sauce.
